Using CRISPR-off to cure HIV

Silencio Bio

more content

The Problem

In 2019, 38 million people globally were living with HIV. Out of that number, 690 000 people have died from AIDS-related illnesses. In that same year, 1.7 million people became newly infected with HIV.

The Status Quo

The only current treatment is antiretroviral therapy, a medication regimen that only slows down progression of HIV, while the immune system continues to weaken. It requires monthly treatment from date of diagnosis and requires a payment of around $2 700 every month.

The Solution

We are going to use a newly developped CRISPR technology called CRISPR-off to silence HIV-1 DNA through DNA methylation. We hypothesize that although the HIV-1 DNA remains in the host cells, the silencing of the DNA would stop the virus from affecting the host.

Other Factors

Here are some more factors and information on the process of bringing our solution to reality

Preclinical Trials

For preclinical trials, we will be using 3 groups of mice and inject them with cells that have been externally injected with HIV. Then we will use our treatment to determine doses and effectiveness.

Clinical Trials

Clinical trials and FDA approval may take 10+ years. Each phase of these trials will determine side effects, effectiveness, and comparisons to antiretroviral therapy.

Cost

Due to the high cost of this solution much like other gene therapies, we will begin our implementation in richer countries such as the United States as they have the infastructure needed for implementation.
